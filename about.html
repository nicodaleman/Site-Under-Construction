Music technology for classical and new music

Bio

Nico Daleman is an audio engineer, musicologist and “music enhancer” who works to expand the sonic possibilities of musicians and the outreach of performing arts organizations. Currently, Nico lives in Boston working as a freelance audio engineer for contemporary the music organizations Non-event and Kadence Arts, and teaching music technology private lessons.

He believes that music technology and classical music do not have to be mutually exclusive and he is always looking to explore scenarios where those two juxtapose and coexist, mainly in collaboration with New Music performers, composers, and organizations. Through the expertize of the music software such as Max-MSP, Nico aims to bring music technology possibilities to classical musicians, helping them to increase their productivity and creativity in order to develop a larger and more diverse audience.

During his time in the United States, Nico has been actively involved in the musical scene through Boston-based organizations such as From the Top, The Boston Children Chorus, and Borromeo String Quartet. He was also an integral part of Sonation, Inc, the developers of Cadenza app, the orchestra that follows you, adding new content and repertoire to the catalogue of pieces. He was also part of the New England Conservatory Audio Visual Services staff–editing, transferring, mixing, recording, and mastering student and faculty recitals. In 2014 he was chosen as a Production Fellow for the 9th Cartagena Festival Internacional de Música, and in 2015 he participated in as an Audio Apprentice in the 39th Spoleto USA Festival, in Charleston, South Carolina.

On the academic level, Nico developed an interest in the influence of recording technologies in the production of classical music, particularly in the HIP movement. His other curiosities include the history of sound recording, music technology influence in American minimalism, electro-acoustic and computer music performance, sound design in cinema as musique concrete, opera and sound, and the exotization of the west.

Nico started his musical career at the age of 9, when he enrolled into Batuta, an El-Sistema like organization, where he studied violin. Nico holds a B.M. in Sound Engineering, from form the Pontificia Universidad Javeriana honoris thesis Compositores Javerianos V: Jazz, with Carlos Albrecht, president of the Verbander Deutscher Tonmeister as his thesis manager; and a M.M. in Musicology from New England Conservatory.
