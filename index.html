About

Nico Daleman is an audio engineer, musicologist and “sonic enhancer” interested in the history and development of music technology and its influence in the performance of classical and contemporary music. His theoretical, technical, and professional interests, mixed (pun intended) with his passion for computer electronic music, sound art, and sound design lead him to find an approach to conciliate these areas, in such a way that they do not have to be opposite or mutually exclusive. As a sound artist, his aim is to become an active part of the performance, not only through the enhancement of musical pieces and/or performances, but through the direct involvement into the development of the performance, in a way that includes musical improvisation, critical sensibility and aesthetical judgment.

Currently based in Cologne, Germany, Nico was actively involved in the musical scene through Boston-based organizations such as From the Top, The Boston Children Chorus, and Borromeo String Quartet. He was also an integral part of Sonation, Inc, the New England Conservatory Audio Visual Services. In 2014 he was chosen as a Production Fellow for the 9th Cartagena Festival Internacional de Música, and in 2015 he participated as an Audio Apprentice in the 39th Spoleto USA Festival, in Charleston, South Carolina.

Nico holds a M.M in Musicology from New England Conservatory and a B.M. in Sound Engineering, from form the Pontificia Universidad Javeriana honoris thesis Compositores Javerianos V: Jazz, with Carlos Albrecht, president of the Verbander Deutscher Tonmeister as his thesis manager.
